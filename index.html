Marian College Kuttikanam · Pathanamthitta, Kerala

MSc Dissertation · Supervised by Dr Robin Cubitt · 2024–25

Regularised Econometrics vs Nonlinear Machine Learning: A Temporal Cross-Validation Framework for Predicting State-Level Child Malnutrition in India

University of Nottingham · School of Economics · MSc Economics and Data Science

Using NFHS-4 and RBI indicators from 2015 to 2016 to forecast NFHS-5 malnutrition measures from 2019 to 2021, this dissertation employed a temporal cross-validation design to rigorously benchmark five econometric and machine learning models against each other across 35 Indian states, validated through both leave-one-state-out and regional holdout strategies.

Key finding Machine learning improved accuracy only for underweight, where Random Forest captured complex interactions. Ridge and LASSO were more reliable for stunting, wasting, and severe wasting demonstrating that regularised econometrics outperforms ML in small-sample policy settings.
Policy insight Education, female literacy, and demographic pressure consistently emerged as the strongest predictors. Income and financial variables played only a marginal role, reaffirming Sen's entitlement framework.
Methods OLS · Ridge · LASSO · Random Forest · XGBoost · LOOCV · SHAP Values · Moran's I · Partial Dependence Plots
Applied Microeconometrics · ECON4008 · Group Project · May 2025

The Impact of Boardroom Gender Diversity on the Gender Pay Gap

University of Nottingham · Co-authored with Divesh Dinesh Aswani, Siddhangana Ray, Florian Burnat

Using firm-level panel data from 2017 to 2023 across 22,399 observations, this group project investigated the causal effect of boardroom gender diversity on the gender pay gap in UK firms, employing a range of propensity score-based estimators to control for confounding firm characteristics including size, profitability, leverage, and industry.

Key finding Above-median female board representation was associated with a statistically significant reduction in the mean gender pay gap of approximately 2.2 percentage points for hourly pay and 10 percentage points for bonus pay. No robust effect was found on the median pay gap, suggesting the impact is concentrated at the upper end of the pay distribution.
Methods Nearest Neighbour Matching · Kernel Matching · IPW · AIPW · Propensity Score Matching · Stata · 22,000+ firm-year records
Big Data Economics · ECON4060 · 2024–25

Forecasting Interbank Overnight Rate and CPI Using Big Data

University of Nottingham · Australian Bureau of Statistics and Reserve Bank of Australia Data

Evaluated machine learning approaches for macroeconomic forecasting using quarterly Australian data from Q2 1985 to Q2 2015 across up to 103 macroeconomic indicators. Compared Ridge Regression and Gradient Boosting for forecasting CPI inflation and interbank overnight rates, assessing not only predictive accuracy but also stability during economic shocks and interpretability for policy use.

Key finding Ridge regression with 20 to 40 predictors yielded the most stable forecasts, consistent with findings from Beckers and Bernardi (2018). Gradient Boosting offered gains in accuracy in some scenarios but at the cost of interpretability. Model selection depends critically on the specific use case and the accuracy versus transparency trade-off.
Methods Ridge Regression · Gradient Boosting · Time-Series Cross-Validation · Rolling Window Backtesting · SHAP Values · Python
Econometric Data Analysis · 2024–25

Stock Market Volatility, Consumer Confidence & Post-Brexit UK Economic Growth

University of Nottingham · MSc Economics and Data Science · Monthly Data July 2016 – June 2024

Using 96 months of monthly data on FTSE 100 volatility, consumer confidence, and GDP growth in post-Brexit UK, I applied a full time series econometric pipeline running from stationarity testing and cointegration analysis through to Vector Error Correction Modelling, in order to separate short-run dynamics from long-run equilibrium relationships. Model stability was validated through eigenvalue analysis, residual diagnostics, and Lagrange-multiplier tests, with impulse transmission explored using Orthogonal Impulse Response Functions.

Key finding FTSE volatility has a significant short-run positive effect on GDP growth, while consumer confidence shows no meaningful short-run impact suggesting post-Brexit economic dynamics are driven more by structural shocks and market forces than by household sentiment.
Long-run result Johansen cointegration confirmed two cointegrating relationships. In the long run, stock market volatility and economic growth maintain a stable positive equilibrium, with consumer confidence playing only a marginal role.
Methods VECM · Johansen Cointegration · ADF Stationarity Testing · Impulse Response Functions · AIC Lag Selection · Residual Diagnostics · Stata
